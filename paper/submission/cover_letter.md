# Cover Letter — Child Abuse & Neglect

[Date]

Dear Editors of *Child Abuse & Neglect*,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript **"Sexual-Violence Victimization,
Household Adversity, and Adolescent Substance Use and Social Withdrawal: A
Design-Aware Machine-Learning Analysis of the 2023 National Youth Risk
Behavior Survey"** for consideration as an empirical report.

The 2023 National YRBS is the first cycle to measure household adverse
childhood experiences, school connectedness, and parental monitoring
alongside its long-standing sexual-violence and substance-use batteries.
Using this sample of 20,103 US high school students, the manuscript makes
four contributions relevant to the journal's readership:

1. **A severity-graded exposure–outcome gradient.** Students reporting
   sexual-violence victimization show elevated prevalence of all twelve
   substance outcomes, with prevalence ratios rising monotonically from 2.2
   (alcohol) to 7.9 (injection drug use) — a pattern consistent with
   escalating trauma-driven coping and difficult to reconcile with
   dispositional risk-taking accounts. Adjusted odds ratios (2.19–3.19)
   are robust to unmeasured confounding at E-values of 2.3–3.0.
2. **A formal mediation test of the coping pathway.** Persistent sadness or
   hopelessness mediates 15–23% of the exposure–substance-use association
   and 46% of the exposure–low-school-connectedness association under the
   potential-outcomes framework — to our knowledge the first such
   decomposition in a nationally representative adolescent sample.
3. **A calibrated, fairness-audited screening prototype.** Using only
   thirteen intake-collectable items (protected attributes excluded from
   inputs, audited for subgroup performance), the model identifies 81% of
   students currently using substances at an operating point supported by
   decision-curve analysis — evidence that trauma-informed early
   identification is feasible with information schools already collect.
4. **A methodological finding of independent value to YRBS analysts:**
   the 20 new-to-2023 items exhibit school-level questionnaire-version
   missingness (83–87% of school clusters all-or-none missing), which we
   document and address with mechanism-based subsample analysis rather
   than imputation.

All analyses use public-use, de-identified CDC microdata; no IRB review was
required. The complete analysis pipeline is openly available and cited in
the manuscript (repository links blinded in the review copy). The
manuscript is approximately 7,500 words with 9 tables and 5 figures, and
includes a plain-language summary. A preprint has been posted to medRxiv
[DOI to be added].

This manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and has

Given the sensitivity of the topic, the manuscript uses risk-factor
